About

Junior Research Fellow in Applied Mathematics at the University of Szeged, Hungary. My research focuses on mathematical and computational modeling of infectious diseases, social contact patterns, and actuarial science.

New: Research Student Fellow (Oct 2025 – Nov 2027) at Amref International University, working on the EU‑funded Migration and Displacement Action Plan for Sub‑Saharan Africa (MAPS). I study migration and forced displacement in Kenya through mixed‑methods fieldwork, survey design, and data analysis in collaboration with local and international supervisors.

Research

Mathematical Epidemiology

Modeling infectious disease dynamics, sensitivity analysis of age-structured contact matrices, and interventions.

Migration & Displacement (MAPS)

Mixed‑methods research on migration and forced displacement in Kenya within the EU‑funded MAPS project: desk research and gap analysis, stakeholder consultations, instrument design (surveys, interviews, FGDs), field data collection, and policy‑relevant analysis.

Actuarial Science & Finance

Quantitative risk modeling, data-driven cluster analysis, and uncertainty quantification in insurance.

Data Science

Machine learning and applied data analysis for complex systems and decision support.
